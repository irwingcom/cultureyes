overview.

Users spend too much time finding optimal activities that reflect their trip through different applications. What if there was an app that offers users activities and events rated by locals so that user can get the experience they're looking for.

user research.

I first created a proto-persona on what i thought the best user would be for the application. Wanted to make sure it would be someone with time to travel, that is looking to get away and for new experiences.
In this research project, I planned and conducted 5 interviews with ideal users for the product. All travelers will have elements in common, frequently travels, age range will all be around the same age as the proto-persona.
The aim of the survey was to know what criteria influences a traveler’s decisions in the selection process; identify people’s thought process when looking for activities to do on a trip; and to know what kind of trip planning experiences the user is looking for. The survey consisted of 10 questions per objective to collect qualitative data.

Questions & Answers

After sorting through the answers I organized the answers into a Affinity Diagram. Splitting the interviewee's answers into five different categories to evaluate the most common features a user is looking for on a trip. I recognized a few recurring topics for the most ideal experience.

-Users mostly Plan Ahead-

-Activities that they cannot do at Home-

-Activities based on the Culture & Region-

-Users likes being able to customize trips with filters on the current applications they use-

-Time, Cost, Location play into past/present/future of trip activities-

-Personal reviews are a Must-

Through user-centric research I created an Ideal User Persona for the App. Someone who loves to travel and experience new cultures, but is stuck mostly traveling for work. So in the small amount of time they have needs to find an activity that will reflect the area they are in. So the question is how might we build an application that will sort through activities and show the best activities that reflect the culture of the area being visited?

competitor analysis.

When looking at what are the main applications out there that offer users activities that are in their are there is Airbnb, Tripadvisor and Yelp. I went through each application trying to find a optimal activity in two different cities. One city was known for tourist attractions while the other was not known too well. Each had their ups and downs, but after a thorough analysis I learned what could be built on.

Strengths

-When going to the airbnb website there is a link where you can click experience. Once clicked local hosts postings show a variety of different activities adjacent to your area of stay. Including price, a map showing where in the city the activity is, and rating with reviews. The posting are from a local host and entails the experience of the activity.
-They also have online experiences where you can pay and watch an activity from your home due to Covid-19.

-Once getting to their website you can log in with google or facebook
-You hit the serach bar and it lready suggests different places to go visit
-Once you enter your city the results show categories as in culture, history, etc.
-Activity gives you full background, info on Covid measures
-They list frequently asked questions and answers about the area
-Very well organized

-Yelp has "Elite Yelpers" they are not paid, they are voluteers known for being the top reviewers.
-You can use list or location when looking for something in your area
